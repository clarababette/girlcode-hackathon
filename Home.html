Premenstrual Dysphoric Disoder (PMDD)

Premenstrual Disphoric Disoder is a much more severe form
of Premenstrual Syndrome(PMS).It may affect women of childbearing age.
It is a severe and chronic medical condition that needs attention and treatement.
Lifestyle changes and sometimes medicines can help manage symptoms.

What causes PMDD?

It is not exactly known why some women
get PMDD. Decreasing levels of estrogen and progesterone
hormones after ovulation and before menstruation may trigger
symptoms. Serotonin, a brain chemical that regulates mood,hunger and sleep,
may also play a role. Serotonin levels, like hormone levels, change throughout your menstrual cycle.

Symptoms of PMDD

PMDD Symptoms include:
Mood swings,Intense anger and conflict
with other people,No interest in your usual activities,
Appetite changes,Headaches,Fatigue,Trouble concentrating.

Ever heard of Spoon Theory?

It is a way of describing the experience of chronic illness
and its limitations using a metaphor. It was created by Christine Miserandino.
The theory uses spoons as a visual way to explain how much energy someone has throughout the day.
The spoons in spoon theory can represent mental or physical energy needed tp do activities or complete tasks.
